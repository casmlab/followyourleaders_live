What is Follow Your Leaders?

Follow Your Leaders is a research project about how elected officials use social media, especially Twitter. We collect tweets from Twitter and combine them with data from other sources like roll call votes, fundraising efforts, and the U.S. Census to show trends and differences in how politicians use social media. We made the site so you could see what politicians are up to as well.

How do we get the data?

We've been collecting data since the summer of 2011 using the Twitter API. We currently use a Python application to retrieve data from Twitter, parse the data, and store it in MongoDB databases. We have used a couple different apps to gather data, and what you see here was collected using purpletag.

We also pull data from other public sources including

  • Congress.org
  • govtrack.us
  • Voteview.com

Who's working on Follow Your Leaders?

Libby Hemphill, an associate professor of information at the University of Michigan School of Information, is the leader of the project. She started it while on the faculty at Illinois Institute of Technology where students in computer science and humanities helped with early designs. The version you see was developed by UMSI Master's Students—Paiju Cheng built much of the site with an assist from Angela Schöpke wrangling data.
